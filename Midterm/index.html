State of Malaria in the Modern World

RISK: About 3.2 billion people – almost half of the world’s population – are at risk of malaria.
CASES: 214 million malaria cases reported worldwide in 2015.
INCIDENCE: 37% global decrease in malaria incidence between 2000 and 2015.
MORTALITY: 60% decrease in global malaria mortality rates between 2000 and 2015.

Progress

An increasing number of countries are moving towards elimination of malaria. Whereas only 13 countries were estimated to have fewer than 1000 malaria cases in 2000, 33 countries are estimated to have achieved this milestone in 2015. Global financing for malaria control increased from an estimated US$ 960 million in 2005 to US$ 2.5 billion in 2014.
